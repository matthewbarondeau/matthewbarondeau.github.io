About Me

I'm a sixth-year Electrical and Computer Engineering Ph.D. candidate at UT Austin, working with Dr. Andreas Gerstlauer in the SLAM Lab. My research focuses on efficient multithreading architectures for near-memory computing, ML-based heterogeneous runtime optimization, and resiliency in parallel systems. I'm particularly interested in microarchitecture design, compiler optimizations, and ML-driven runtime reconfiguration. I'll be starting as a Resiliency and Safety Architect at Nvidia in December 2026.

I've taught embedded systems courses at both undergraduate and graduate levels, and I mentor first-year ECE students through UT's First-Year Interest Group program. Beyond academia, I enjoy science fiction and fantasy novels, soccer, running, hiking, and baking.
