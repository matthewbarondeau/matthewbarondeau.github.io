About Me

I'm a Resiliency and Safety Architect at Nvidia in Austin, TX, working on GPU datacenter resiliency and automotive safety. Before joining full-time, I completed three internships at Nvidia across GPU architecture, L2 cache design, and resiliency analysis.

I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from UT Austin in 2026, advised by Dr. Andreas Gerstlauer in the SLAM Lab. My dissertation, Efficient Scheduling in Same-ISA Heterogeneous Systems, focused on ML-based runtime optimization and near-memory computing architectures. During my time at UT, I was a Qualcomm Innovation Fellow, received the Texas ECE Graduate Leadership Award, and taught courses ranging from embedded systems to compilers. I also mentored first-year ECE students as a First-Year Interest Group instructor.

Outside of work, I'm an endurance runner and hiker. I enjoy reading, mostly science fiction and fantasy, with a particular fondness for Brandon Sanderson's Cosmere, Robert Jordan's Wheel of Time, and the Star Wars Expanded Universe. I enjoy soccer and am an MNUFC supporter. When I'm not running or lost in a novel, I enjoy planning a move to Minnesota, reading up on home efficiency improvements (especially geothermal heat pumps), and cooking and baking.
