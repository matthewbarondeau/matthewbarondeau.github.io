About Me

I'm a fourth year Electrical and Computing Engineering Ph.D. student working under Dr. Andreas Gerstlauer in the SLAM Lab at UT Austin. In my research, I have previously looked at multithreading architectures to improve throughput and hide memory latency and am now examining heterogeneous runtime improvements and multithreading improvements for cloud workloads. Outside of research, I have spent a lot of time in industry internships working with memory systems at Nvidia and Arm. In my 'copious' free time, I am fascinated by issue stage microarchitecture and improving backend resource utilization in Out-of-Order processors.

Outside of computer architecture, I enjoy mentoring students, at both the graduate and undergraduate level, and helping them adjust to academia. When I am not working or volunteering at UT, I like to read, play soccer, bake, and volunteer down at the Central Texas Food Bank.
